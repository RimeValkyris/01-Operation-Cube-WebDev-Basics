Flannan Isles: Marcus Article

Analysis Paralysis

Marcus reads everything, from the ingredients on product labels to the changing expressions of important figures. However, the sheer breadth of information that her keen eyes observe brings her considerable trouble. She frequently finds herself caught in a dilemma of "this makes sense, but so does that, often overthinking to the point of immobility. The information she absorbs does not aid her decision-making; in fact, it hinders her.

Record Everything

Before joining the Foundation, Marcus had spent a long time stationed in the Flannan Isles. She was captivated by the mysterious incidents that occurred there and resolved to record every one of them, from the pervasive sea fog to the twisted reefs, the eternally operating lighthouse, and the disappearing people. She had to capture every detail. They were all just too significant to be discarded. Ultimately, Marcus wrote it all down, producing an extensive and detailed manuscript. She showed the people what she saw, eagerly anticipating their reactions.

Her Path

After writing History of the Flannan Isles, Marcus followed Hofmann to the Foundation. With her mentor's help, she no longer had to bear the burden of making decisions alone, nor did she need to handle complex interpersonal relationships. Hofmann turned the pages of the world for her; all she had to do was concentrate on what she did best-reading. But as the curtain fell over Vienna, she could no longer turn the pages of that burning old book. Now, she must once again read this complex, terrifying, and beautiful world alone. The path before her is the only one left. It is solitary, long, and arduous. But she will keep moving forward.
